# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

PatientMatch is intended to support public-good clinical trial access. We want participation in this project to be respectful, patient-centered, and privacy-conscious.

We pledge to make participation in this project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language.
- Respecting patient privacy and avoiding personal health disclosures in public threads.
- Being careful with medical claims and avoiding eligibility guarantees.
- Giving and receiving constructive technical feedback.
- Prioritizing accessibility, safety, and clarity in patient-facing work.

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, intimidation, or discriminatory language.
- Publishing private communications or personal information without consent.
- Asking contributors or users to disclose personal health information in public.
- Making unsupported medical, enrollment, or treatment claims.
- Other conduct that could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional open-source project.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may remove content, reject contributions, or restrict participation when behavior violates this code of conduct or creates privacy, safety, or trust risks.

Security and privacy concerns should be reported privately using the contact in `SECURITY.md`.
